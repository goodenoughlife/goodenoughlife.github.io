Background

Since 2018, I have been on a mission to accomplish a (seemingly) simple thing: live a good enough life. After a childhood filled with trauma and teenage years dominated by SI, I found myself, 21, in a predicament; I WANTED to live, but bouts of severe anxiety, dread, self-doubt, inability to focus, paranoia, and intense mood swings had overtaken my day to day life. I dedicated most of 2019 to stabilizing through psychodynamic therapy, meditation, yoga, fellowship, and reading psychology, sociology and neuroscience literature. In 2020 during the global pandemic, I entered a relationship that helped bring my stability to new heights and I paused my healing journey. Fast foward to the end of 2023, and that relationship ended in a situation mirroring the trauma of my childhood, and I found myself unstable again for the first time in years. This website is documentation of part 2 of my healing journey.

Trauma Disorders

Anxiety? Depression? OCD? Borderline Personality Disorder? Bipolar Disorder? ADHD? Autism?..... CPTSD ? I have never felt a strong desire to be seen as having a specific mental health disorder. I identify as a "Long Term Trauma Survivor With A Cluster Fuck of Trauma Symptoms", which appears to be best represented in the (very limited) CPTSD literature. I do not believe any modality of therapy is the golden solution, and every statistic points to the client-therapist relationship being the most important factor in progress. A part of me yearns to understand what each of the never-ending trauma modality acronyms (CBT,DBT,ACT,EMDR,SE,EFT,TIST,IFS) can offer, yet holistic approaches & non-sponsored and evidence-based (and what even is evidence?) information is almost impossible to come by. My goal is to research the over-arching themes. My goal is to be able to explain what I learn to somebody who has never picked up a psychology book. My goal is to heal; to share this information and these free resources.
