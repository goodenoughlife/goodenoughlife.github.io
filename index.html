Background

Since 2018, I have been on a mission to accomplish a (seemingly) simple thing: live a good enough life. After a childhood filled with trauma, I found myself, 21, in a predicament; I WANTED to live, but bouts of severe anxiety, dread, self-doubt, inability to focus, paranoia, and intense mood swings had overtaken my day to day life. I dedicated most of 2019 to stabilizing through psychodynamic therapy, meditation, yoga, fellowship, and psych literature. In 2020 during the global pandemic, I entered a relationship that helped bring my stability to new heights. Fast foward to the end of 2023, and that relationship ended in a situation mirroring the trauma of my childhood, and I found myself unstable again. This website is documentation of part 2 of my healing journey.

Current Approach

I am currently exploring Janina Fisher's structural dissociation model, which is the central focus of her TSIT modality. The goal is to get the client to unblend with their traumatized parts and to allow the "normal life self" to act as a compassionate moderator for each of the parts, valuing their respective usefulness at a point in time. With a focus on sensorimotor techniques, she aims for clients to gain earned secure attachment through reparenting of traumatized parts.
